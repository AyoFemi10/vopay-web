*Last updated: January 1, 2025*

## 1. Purpose

This KYC Policy describes how VOPayX verifies customer identities to comply with regulatory requirements and prevent financial crime.

## 2. Regulatory Basis

Our KYC program is based on:

- CBN Know Your Customer (KYC) Manual
- NFIU Guidance Notes
- Money Laundering (Prevention and Prohibition) Act 2022

## 3. KYC Tiers

### Tier 1 — Basic Verification

**Required documents:**
- Valid Nigerian phone number
- Bank Verification Number (BVN)

**Acceptable limits:** See [Limits & Fees](/docs/user/limits-fees)

### Tier 2 — Standard Verification

**Required documents (one of):**
- National Identity Card (NIN slip or card)
- International Passport (data page)
- Voter's Card
- Driver's Licence

### Tier 3 — Full Verification

**Required documents:**
- Tier 2 documents
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement dated within 3 months)
- Liveness check / selfie with document

## 4. Business KYC

Business accounts require:

- CAC Certificate of Incorporation
- Memorandum and Articles of Association
- Director(s) identification (Tier 2 KYC for each director)
- Proof of business address
- Board resolution (for accounts above ₦10,000,000 limit)

## 5. Verification Process

1. Customer submits documents via the app
2. Automated checks via Smile Identity (liveness, document authenticity)
3. Manual review for flagged submissions
4. Outcome communicated within 24–48 hours

## 6. Re-verification

We may request re-verification when:

- Documents expire
- Account activity changes significantly
- We suspect a document was fraudulent
- Regulatory requirement changes

## 7. Rejected Applications

Common rejection reasons:

| Reason | Resolution |
|--------|------------|
| Blurry or incomplete document | Retake photo in good lighting |
| Expired document | Submit a valid, current document |
| Name mismatch with BVN | Contact support with explanation |
| Failed liveness check | Retry in a well-lit environment |

## 8. Data Protection

All identity documents are:
- Encrypted at rest (AES-256)
- Transmitted over TLS 1.3
- Accessed only by authorised compliance staff
- Retained for 5 years after account closure
